<p><ul><li>Radial</li><li>Axial</li><li>Radial-axial</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Expansion turbines are classified into three main market types: radial, axial, and radial-axial turbines. Radial turbines have a design that directs gas flow perpendicularly to the turbine shaft, ensuring efficient energy extraction in compact spaces. Axial turbines allow gas flow parallel to the shaft, making them suitable for high-flow applications where efficiency at larger scales is crucial. Radial-axial turbines combine elements of both designs, offering versatility for medium flow rates and compact designs while maximizing performance across various operational conditions.</p></p>
