<p><p>The cattle vaccines market comprises several types, including live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and other formulations. Live attenuated vaccines use weakened pathogens to stimulate immunity, providing strong protection. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, ensuring safety but may require boosters for lasting immunity. Other vaccines encompass newer technologies, such as recombinant or subunit vaccines, which use specific antigens to provoke immune responses with fewer side effects. Together, these options cater to diverse vaccination needs in livestock management.</p></p>
