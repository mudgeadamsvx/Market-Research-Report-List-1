<p><p>The polymeric pigment dispersants market is primarily segmented into two types: homopolymers and copolymers. Homopolymers consist of a single type of monomer repeated throughout the polymer chain, providing uniformity and stability in pigment dispersion. In contrast, copolymers are composed of two or more different monomers, allowing for tailored properties such as improved compatibility and enhanced performance in various applications. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing pigment dispersion, improving stability, and enhancing the overall quality of paints, inks, and coatings.</p></p>
