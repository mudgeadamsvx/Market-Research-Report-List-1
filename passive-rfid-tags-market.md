<p><p>Passive RFID (Radio Frequency Identification) tags are electronic devices that consist of a microchip and an antenna, enabling them to transmit data back to a reader when powered by radio waves emitted from the reader itself. Unlike active RFID tags, which have their own power source, passive tags derive their energy from the interrogation signal of the reader, making them cost-effective and lightweight. These tags are commonly used in various applications, including inventory management, asset tracking, and supply chain logistics.</p><p>The Passive RFID Tags Market is experiencing notable growth, driven by innovations in technology and increasing demand for efficient tracking solutions across industries such as retail, healthcare, and manufacturing. <p><p>The passive RFID tags market is primarily segmented into two types: inlays and hard tags. Both types are widely used in logistics, retail, and inventory management.</p></p>
