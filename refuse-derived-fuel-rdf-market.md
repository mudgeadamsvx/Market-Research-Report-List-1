<p><p>Refuse-Derived Fuel (RDF) is produced from municipal solid waste and is used as an alternative fuel source. The market is divided into two main types: Dense RDF and Loose RDF. Dense RDF involves compacted waste, which offers higher energy density and easier transport, making it suitable for industrial use. In contrast, Loose RDF consists of shredded, non-compacted waste, providing a more flexible option for combustion in power generation. Both types aim to reduce landfill usage and promote energy recovery.</p></p>
